# Chapter 2: Literature Review
## 2.1 Existing Systems
Currently, information on bus routes in Dhaka is disseminated through a fragmented mix of sources, including:
- **Manually Painted Bus Signs:** Buses have signs (often in Bengali) indicating their routes, but these can be difficult to read, non-standardized, and not comprehensive for all stops.
- **Word-of-Mouth:** The most common method where passengers rely on local shopkeepers, rickshaw pullers, or fellow commuters for information. This is highly unreliable.
- **Online Forums and Social Media:** Platforms like Facebook groups are often used to ask for route information, but these are not structured and require time to get a response.
- **Mobile Applications:** There are a few attempts at bus route apps, but they are often outdated, have limited coverage, or are not user-friendly. Few are actively maintained.
## 2.2 Related Applications
Several applications exist globally and locally that attempt to solve similar problems.
- **Google Maps:** This is the most widely used global navigation app. It provides public transit directions, including bus routes in many cities. However, its coverage for Dhaka's intricate bus network is often incomplete or inaccurate, especially for local routes and fares. It is a general-purpose navigation tool, not specifically tailored for the unique characteristics of the Dhaka bus system.
- **Pathao/Uber:** While primarily ride-hailing services, they have attempted to add public transit features, but they are not focused on a comprehensive, free-to-use public bus information system.

## 2.4 Strengths and Weaknesses of Existing Approaches
**Strengths:**
**- Google Maps:** Its global mapping and routing algorithms are powerful. Its map interface is highly polished and familiar to users.
- **Social Media:** Offers a source of community knowledge and can sometimes provide updates on temporary route changes.
**Weaknesses:**
- **Google Maps**: Lacks localized, detailed bus stop and fare data for Dhaka. It is not designed to manage the specific complexities of the city's bus system.
- **Social Media:** The information is unstructured, not verifiable, and requires significant effort to extract. It's reactive rather than proactive.
- **Word-of-Mouth:** Highly unreliable and prone to error.
- **General Absence:** A single, reliable, and up-to-date source of bus information for Dhaka does not exist.
## 2.5 Research Gap
The analysis of existing systems reveals a clear gap: there is no dedicated, reliable, and easy-to-use platform that provides comprehensive bus route and fare information for Dhaka city. Google Maps, while a powerful tool, lacks the detailed, localized data specific to Dhaka's bus network. Other scattered sources are either unreliable or lack a structured database. This project aims to fill this gap by creating a dedicated solution that combines a user-friendly interface with a robust, manageable data system.
## 2.6 Why this Project is Needed
This project is needed to bridge the information gap in Dhaka's public transport system. It is a direct response to the everyday struggles of millions of citizens. By providing a reliable, on-demand service, the app will:
1.	**Empower Commuters:** Give them control over their travel decisions.
2.	**Improve Efficiency:** Reduce time wasted on finding the right bus.
3.	**Enhance Transparency:** Provide clear, upfront fare information.
4.	**Digitize a Key Public Service:** Contribute to the modernization of the city.
5.	**Serve as a Foundation:** Create a platform that can be expanded with future features like live tracking and analytics.
